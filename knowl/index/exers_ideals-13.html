Exercise 13.

An important ideal that arises in commutative algebra are annihilator ideals. Let \(R\) be a commutative ring and \(r\in R\) an element. Define the annihilator of \(r\) to be the set
\begin{equation*} \mathrm{Ann}_R(r) = \{s\in R\mid sr = 0\}. \end{equation*}
Prove that \(\mathrm{Ann}_R(r)\) is always an ideal.
