Exercise 14.

Another important ideal that arises in commutative algebra are radical ideals. Let \(I\subseteq R\) be an ideal in a commutative ring. Define the radica of \(r\) to be the set
\begin{equation*} \sqrt{I} = \{a\in R\mid a^n\in I\text{ for some }n\in\N\}. \end{equation*}
Prove that \(\sqrt{I}\) is an ideal.
