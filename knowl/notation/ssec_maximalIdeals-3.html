Definition 3.4.12.

Let \(R\) be commutative and let \(\mathfrak{m}\subsetneq R\) be a non-zero proper ideal. We say that \(\mathfrak{m}\) is a maximal ideal if no proper ideal of \(R\) properly contains \(\mathfrak{m}\text{.}\) That is, if \(J\) is an ideal satisfying \(\mathfrak{m}\subseteq J\subseteq R\text{,}\) either \(J=\mathfrak{m}\) or \(J=R\text{.}\)
