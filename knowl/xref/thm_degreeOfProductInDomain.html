Theorem 2.3.11.

Let \(R\) be a domain, and let \(p(x),q(x)\in R[x]\) be nonzero polynomials. Then \(\deg(p(x)\cdot q(x)) = \deg(p(x)) + \deg(q(x))\text{.}\)

Proof.

Let the leading term of \(p(x)\) be \(a_n x^n\) and the leading term of \(q(x)\) be \(b_m x^m\text{.}\) Then the leading term of \(p(x) q(x)\) is \(a_n b_m x^{n+m}\text{.}\) (Observe that since \(R\) is a domain, \(a_n b_m \ne 0\text{.}\)) Thus, \(\deg(p(x) q(x)) = n+m = \deg(p(x)) + \deg(q(x))\text{.}\)
