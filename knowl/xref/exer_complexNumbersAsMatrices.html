Exercise 3.

Consider the set
\begin{equation*} L = \left\{\begin{bmatrix} a \amp -b \\ b \amp a \end{bmatrix}\mid a,b\in\mathbb{R}\right\}. \end{equation*}
Prove that \(L\) is a field with the usual addition and multiplication of matrices.
  1. Prove that \(L\) is a field with the usual addition and multiplication of matrices.
  2. Is there an element \(A\in L\) such that \(A^2=-I_2\) (the \(2\times2\) identity matrix)? That is, can you solve the equation \(x^2+1=0\) in \(L\text{?}\)
