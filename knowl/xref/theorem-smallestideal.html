Theorem 3.2.11.

Given a commutative ring \(R\) and a subset \(S\) of \(R\text{,}\) \(\ideal{S}\) is the smallest ideal containing \(S\) in the sense that, if \(J\) is any ideal of \(R\) containing \(S\text{,}\) \(\ideal{S}\subseteq J\text{.}\)

Proof.

Let \(I\) be any ideal containing \(S\text{.}\) Thus, \(I\) is one of the ideals on the right-hand side of (3.2.1). Since \(\ideal{S}\) is formed by the intersection of \(I\) with other ideals, \(\ideal{S}\subseteq I\text{.}\)
