Exercise 13. Correspondence Theorem.

Prove the weak Correspondence Theorem: For any ideals \(I\subseteq R\) and \(\bar{J}\subseteq \bar{R}=R/I\text{,}\) there exists a unique ideal \(I\subseteq J\subseteq R\) such that the image of \(J\) under the quotient \(\pi\colon R\to \bar{R}\) is \(\bar{J}\text{.}\) Hint: Set
\begin{equation*} J = \pi^{-1}(J) = \{r\in R\mid \pi(r)\in J\}. \end{equation*}
Show that \(J\) is an ideal of \(R\text{,}\) that it contains \(I\text{,}\) that \(\pi(J) = \bar{J}\text{.}\) Then prove that \(J\) is unique.
