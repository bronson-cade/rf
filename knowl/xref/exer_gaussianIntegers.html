Exercise 3. Gaussian Integers.
The Gaussian Integers are the subset of \(\C\) given by
\begin{equation*} \Z[i] = \{a+bi\in \C\mid a,b,\in\Z\}. \end{equation*}
  1. Using the usual addition and multiplication in \(\C\text{,}\) prove that the Gaussian Integers are a ring (don’t appeal to Theorem 2.2.18).
  2. Use the Theorem 2.2.18 to prove that the Gaussian Integers are a ring.
