Example 3.4.13.

The ideal \((x,2)\) is a prime ideal of \(\Z[x]\text{.}\) Indeed, the mapping \(\varphi\colon \Z[x]\to \mathbb{F}_2\) defined by
\begin{equation*} \varphi(a_0+a_1x+\cdots+a_nx^n) = \bar{a}_0 \end{equation*}
is an epimorphism (exercise). This is nothing more than the reduce the constant term modulo 2 mapping.
The kernel of this mapping certainly contains \((x,2)\) as polynomials in \((x,2)\) are nothing more than the polynomials with even constant term, which reduces to 0 modulo 2. Any element not in \((x,2)\) has odd constant term. Thus it will not be in the kernel. Hence, \(\ker(\varphi) = (x,2)\) and by the Noether Isomorphism Theorem \(\Z[x]/(x,2)\cong\mathbb{F}_2\text{.}\) This is a field and so a domain. Thus \((x,2)\) is a prime ideal that is not principal.
